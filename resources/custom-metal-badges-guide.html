Buying guide

Custom metal badges guide for buyers and distributors.

Use this guide to brief a factory on custom metal badges, personalized metal badges, metal badge logos and metal pin badges before requesting a quote.

Good custom metal badges start with clear decisions. The factory needs to know the badge size, base metal, process, plating, color, back attachment, packaging and order quantity. When these details are missing, quotes vary widely and samples can miss the buyer's expectations.

Choose the badge process first

Die struck metal badges are ideal for clean logos, official emblems and antique finishes. Soft enamel badges use raised metal borders and recessed color. Hard enamel badges are polished smooth and feel more premium. Zinc alloy casting works well for irregular shapes, deep relief and larger badge designs.

Pick plating to match the market

Gold and nickel plating are common for retail and corporate badges. Antique brass, antique silver and antique copper make relief details more visible. Black nickel and matte plating can make a modern metal badge logo feel more distinctive.

Back attachment changes the user experience

Butterfly clutch is cost-efficient for metal pin badges. Safety pin backs are common on larger badges. Magnetic backs are useful for uniforms because they avoid holes in clothing. Screw posts and adhesive backs are better for signs, bags and hard goods.

What to send for a quote

Send artwork, target size, quantity, plating preference, enamel colors, backing type and packaging. If you do not know the right process, send reference photos and the intended use. The factory can recommend a production method for your custom metal badges.
