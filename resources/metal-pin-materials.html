Material guide

How to choose materials for custom metal pins.

Metal pin, metal lapel pins and custom metal pins can look very different depending on the base metal, enamel process and finish.

Soft enamel metal pins

Soft enamel pins are a strong default for custom metal pins because they balance price, color and detail. The raised metal lines protect recessed enamel areas and create a tactile surface.

Hard enamel lapel pins

Hard enamel is polished flat after color filling. It is common for premium metal lapel pins, corporate gifts and retail pins where a smooth surface matters.

Die struck metal pins

Die struck pins use metal relief without enamel. Antique plating makes the design visible and gives the pin a traditional coin-like finish.

Zinc alloy, brass and iron

Zinc alloy suits larger or complex shapes. Brass gives sharper stamped detail and a premium feel. Iron is efficient for many standard enamel pin badge orders.
